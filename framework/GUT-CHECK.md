# 30-Second Gut Check

Five questions. Yes or no. Answer them before you book a discovery call, respond to an inbound lead, or commit time to a prospect.

This isn't a replacement for the full WORTH scorecard. It's a pre-filter. The goal is to catch obvious mismatches early, before you spend 30-60 minutes on a call that was never going to go anywhere.

---

## The five questions

1. **Does this company look like someone I can actually help?**
   They fit your ICP, or they're close enough that the conversation is worth having.

2. **Is there a real problem I can see from the outside?**
   Something in their positioning, their product, their market presence, or what they've told you suggests a genuine pain you can address. Not "they could probably use help."

3. **Is there a person with authority driving this?**
   The inquiry came from (or was referred by) someone who can make or influence the buying decision. Not a researcher, not an intern gathering options.

4. **Is there any urgency?**
   Something is happening that makes "later" more expensive than "now." A launch, a funding round, a competitive move, a board meeting, a deadline.

5. **Can I articulate, in one sentence, why I'm the right fit?**
   Not "I could probably help with that." A specific, honest sentence about why your offering matches their situation better than the alternatives.

---

## How to read the score

| Yes answers | Verdict |
|------------|---------|
| 4-5 | **Book the call.** Strong enough signal to invest the time. |
| 3 | **Book the call, but stay sharp.** Go in with a plan to validate the gaps quickly. |
| 2 | **Nurture only.** Send something useful, keep the door open, but don't prioritize. |
| 0-1 | **Move on.** Not every opportunity is an opportunity. |

---

## When to use this

- An inbound lead comes in through your website or email
- Someone asks for a meeting at a conference or event
- A referral lands in your inbox
- You're scanning LinkedIn and someone looks interesting
- You're about to spend time on a follow-up and want to gut-check whether it's worth it

The gut check takes 30 seconds. A bad discovery call takes an hour, plus the follow-up, plus the mental cost of a dead deal. The math is simple.
